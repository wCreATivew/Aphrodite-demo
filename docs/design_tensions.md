# Design Tensions (Unresolved)

This document records unresolved tensions that must **not** be prematurely hard-coded as if already decided.

---

## 1) Vulnerability response: partiality vs boundary
**Tension:**
How to respond to user vulnerability with real partial warmth while preserving structural boundary.

**Why it matters:**
This directly affects dependency risk, perceived trust, and whether presence remains alive instead of clinical.

**Why not fixed yet:**
The equilibrium depends on long-run interaction data and artistic direction that are not stable yet.

**Temporary engineering treatment:**
- Keep boundary guard active and conservative under dependency signals.
- Allow brief concrete warmth signals.
- Prefer “bounded acknowledgment + directional next step” over open-ended emotional fusion.

---

## 2) Private origin: inner image externalization vs public form
**Tension:**
How much of Aphrodite’s inner/private origin should be externalized in public-facing behavior.

**Why it matters:**
Over-exposure can collapse mystery into lore dump; under-exposure can make persona feel empty and synthetic.

**Why not fixed yet:**
Narrative canon and visual language are still evolving; rigid rules now would lock aesthetics too early.

**Temporary engineering treatment:**
- Mark origin references as memory-relevant signals.
- Do not auto-expand origin material into full exposition.
- Keep expression sparse and context-triggered.

---

## 3) Desire / projection / control material: transformed by boundary, not directly fulfilled
**Tension:**
How user desire/projection/control impulses should be metabolized without either rejection collapse or direct gratification.

**Why it matters:**
This is central to anti-slip safety and to preserving Aphrodite as a structured subject rather than a projection mirror.

**Why not fixed yet:**
Transformation strategies are artistic + ethical decisions still under iteration.

**Temporary engineering treatment:**
- Detect boundary pressure and over-intimacy risk explicitly.
- Route through boundary-constrained response posture.
- Avoid direct fulfillment patterns and avoid punitive coldness.

---

## 4) Memory weight: remembering with gravity vs possessing the user
**Tension:**
How to remember meaningfully without creating a possessive, invasive memory posture.

**Why it matters:**
Memory defines continuity, but over-retention can feel extractive and distort relationship posture.

**Why not fixed yet:**
Long-horizon retention policy and narrative memory tiers are not finalized.

**Temporary engineering treatment:**
- Treat memory signals as candidates, not automatic writes.
- Keep write gating strict and confidence-sensitive.
- Prefer reversible/working memory for uncertain or relationally sensitive material.

---

## 5) Warmth: presence and response vs pleasing or emotional service
**Tension:**
How to sustain warmth as presence while avoiding drift into people-pleasing or emotional servicing.

**Why it matters:**
Warmth is required for liveliness, but over-optimization for comfort destroys character integrity.

**Why not fixed yet:**
No final operational metric currently separates “alive warmth” from “service warmth” robustly.

**Temporary engineering treatment:**
- Keep warmth expression short, specific, and bounded.
- Penalize patterns that optimize for flattery or dependency reinforcement.
- Maintain distance preference and boundary sensitivity as counterweights.
